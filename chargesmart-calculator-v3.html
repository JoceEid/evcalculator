How ChargeSmart works

Savings depend on when you charge

ChargeSmart is not a flat discount. Your cost goes up or down based on time of day — and a $4 monthly credit is yours just for participating.

🌙

Charge overnight

Plugging in between 11pm and 7am locks in the lowest rate — around $0.09/kWh. That's where your savings live.

~$0.09/kWh
💳

Monthly credit

Every enrolled month you receive a $4 bill credit — regardless of when you charge. It's your guaranteed baseline.

$4/month guaranteed
☀️

Avoid daytime charging

Charging midday or early evening costs significantly more — up to $0.20/kWh — and can erase your savings entirely.

~$0.20/kWh
💡

The key insight: Charging at the right time can lower your monthly EV cost by $10–$18 vs. standard rate. Charging at the wrong time can cost you more. Your behavior is the variable.

How to succeed

Three habits that protect your grade

One behavioral shift is all it takes to move from a D to an A.

01

Plug in after 11pm

The preferred window runs 11pm to 7am. Start your charge at bedtime and wake up to a full battery at the lowest possible rate.

11:00 pm – 7:00 am
02

Skip the midday top-up

Even a short charge at peak hours adds up. If you need to top up during the day, keep it brief and make up the difference overnight.

Minimize 9am – 11pm charging
03

Use your car's scheduler

Most EVs let you set a charge start time in the vehicle app. Set it for 11:05pm once and the savings happen automatically every night.

Set it once, save every month
