How ChargeSmart works

Night charging pays. Day charging costs more.

ChargeSmart rewards you for charging during off-peak hours with a per-kWh credit, plus a monthly enrollment credit just for being in the program.

🌙

Earn overnight credits

Charging between 11pm and 7am earns a small credit on your bill — roughly $0.01–$0.02 per kWh of usage during that window. Small per charge, meaningful over a month.

Preferred window: 11pm – 7am
💳

Enrollment credit

You receive a $4 monthly credit for your first 12 months enrolled — regardless of when you charge. It's guaranteed money back just for participating.

$4/month · first 12 months
☀️

Daytime charging costs more

Charging between 11am and 7pm increases your exposure to peak demand charges — which can reduce or offset the credits you've earned overnight.

Avoid 11am – 7pm when possible
💡

The bottom line: Overnight charging earns you credits. Daytime charging can erode them. The more consistent your overnight charging, the more you save — and the higher your grade.

How to succeed

Three habits that protect your grade

One behavioral shift is all it takes to move from a D to an A.

01

Plug in after 11pm

The preferred window runs 11pm to 7am. This earns an off-peak credit and keeps your EV load completely outside the peak demand measurement window.

11:00 pm – 7:00 am
02

Avoid stacking appliances at peak hours

Your peak demand is based on total household load. Running the EV, dryer, and AC simultaneously between 11am–7pm creates your worst-case reading — avoid that combination.

Stacking = higher peak reading
03

Use your car's charge scheduler

Most EVs let you schedule a charge start time in the vehicle app. Set it for 11:05pm — the car handles it every night automatically, no willpower required.

Automate it — set once, benefit monthly
